## Crystal Clock Tower
> A narrow clock tower, shining like glass, rises from the center of a misty courtyard. The hour and minute hands on its face show the same second again and again. With every tick, the water on the ground leaps upward for an instant.
- There is no normal wind in the courtyard; the air moves only when the clock ticks.
- The tower door stands ajar, but no warm light comes from inside.

`This event is meant to show the renderer's main read-aloud text, bullet list, DM note, and right-column modules together.`

### Skill Checks
General:
- Passive Perception:
> 10: The tower's sound can be heard at the same volume from every part of the courtyard.
> 15: The ticking does not grow louder as you get closer; its source seems tied more to time than to place.
- At First Glance:
> There are shards of broken glass and dried ink marks on the door's threshold.
- Investigation:
> 12: The glass shards were broken inward, not outward.
> 16: The ink marks show that a hastily drawn warning in front of the door was erased.
> 20: At the end of the erased text, the phrase "let the minute finish" can be made out.
- Arcana:
> 13: There is active magic in the tower, but it is not hostile; it is holding a loop in place.
> 18: If the loop breaks, whatever is hidden inside may be released.
- Religion:
> 15: The joined hour and minute hands are being used not as a symbol of worship, but like a seal diagram.


### Object: Cracked Clock Door
> The door looks as if it is made of dark glass. It has no handle, only three thin rings and an empty scratch beneath each ring.

Checks:
- Investigation:
> 12: The rings were made to be vibrated, not pulled.
> 15: There are small scratches beside the rings; one was made with a fingernail, one with a knife, and one with a metal point.
- Performance:
> 14: Striking the rings in different rhythms makes the door briefly turn transparent.
Loot:
- 3 small silver screws
- Broken pocket watch cover
- Thin metal strip inscribed with "The final minute is a debt"

### item: Backflowing Hourglass
Type: Wondrous Item
Rarity: 2
Attunement: Required
> The black sand in the upper chamber of this palm-sized hourglass flows upward. Its glass is cold, and when viewed up close, the grains inside shine like tiny stars.
Properties:
- Once per long rest, after failing a Dexterity saving throw, you can reroll the die.
- You must use the new result.
- When used, the hourglass turns completely black for 1 minute.

### NPC: Miren, Lost Clockmaker
Side: R
Personality:
- Polite, scattered, and repeats the same sentence in different tones.
- When speaking about time, they never take their eyes off the clock tower.
Opening Dialogue:
> "You are late. No, you are early. Actually, both became the same thing."
If Asked:
> "There is a minute hidden inside the tower. If that minute ends, everyone waiting here will remember all the time they did not age at once."
What They Know:
- The mechanism inside the door opens with three key sounds.
- The key sounds do not come from physical keys, but from actions performed in the correct order.
What They Do Not Know:
- They do not remember who first entered the loop.
Clock Tower:
- "What is inside the tower?"
> "A room, a table, a bell, and a final minute that never ends."
- "Why are you here?"
> "Because someone had to stay outside. I think that someone was me."
Checks:
- Insight:
> 12: Miren is not lying, but their memories are not arriving in order.
> 16: What they fear is not the tower itself, but the completion of the minute inside.
- Medicine:
> 14: They do not look old, but their pulse is irregular; their heart sometimes seems to repeat a beat.

### Unexpected:
Side: R
- If the players try to break the door: the glass does not break; instead, the ticking in the courtyard speeds up for one round.
- If they attack Miren: Miren is harmed, but returns to their old place at the next tick.
- If they leave the tower without opening the door: they exit the courtyard, but begin the next scene feeling 1 minute late.
Failed check:
- Failed Investigation: the door can still be examined; only the correct order of the rings remains unclear.

## The Final Minute Inside
> The door turns transparent, and the moment you step inside, the mist in the courtyard disappears. You find yourself in a narrow room with a high ceiling. In the center of the room is a large table, on the table is a brass bell, and behind the bell is a shadow of sand flowing in reverse.

- Everything inside appears clear; nothing trembles except the shadow.
- The shelves on the walls hold hundreds of blank labels.

`This event is meant to show the combat card, combat moved to the right column, echo, and the second skill check panel.`

### Skill Checks
- At First Glance:
> The bell has no clapper; touching it may not be enough to make it ring.
- Arcana:
> 12: The sand shadow is not alive, but it reacts.
> 17: If the bell rings, the shadow will take shape for a short time.
- History:
> 14: The blank labels resemble an archive system; names may have been stored here.
> 18: The blank labels suggest the records were not erased, but have not yet been written.

### Combat: Minute Shadow
Side: R
> The bell trembles on its own. The black sand behind the table whips upward and becomes a faceless, human-sized shadow.

`This fight should be brief; its purpose is to show the combat card's subsections.`

Stat:
- AC 13 | HP 32 | Speed 9m
- Attack: Shadow Touch +5, 1d8 necrotic
- Weak save: Wisdom
Tactics:
- It moves toward the character who spoke most recently.
- When reduced to half health, it tries to protect the bell.
Special Mechanic:
- At the start of each round, the clock in the room ticks once.
- On the third tick, the shadow teleports to its previous position.
Checks:
- Arcana:
> 13: The shadow feeds not on the bell, but on the sound the bell has not completed.
> 18: If the bell is silenced, the shadow cannot attack for 1 round.
- Insight:
> 15: The shadow is not angry; it acts like a remnant trying to finish its task.


### Unexpected:
Side: R
- If the players ring the bell: the Minute Shadow appears immediately, or if combat has already begun, it grows stronger for 1 round.
- If the players wrap the bell in sand to silence it: the shadow does not attack for 1 round, then burns the sand away.
- If the players speak with the shadow: it only repeats the sentence "the minute must be completed."
Failed check:
- Failed Arcana: the shadow's source cannot be understood, but combat or progress is not blocked.

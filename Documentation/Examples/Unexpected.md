# Unexpected Examples

### Unexpected: Bridge Complications
- If the party calls back to the voices: the water answers with one true memory from the speaker, then a false direction.
- If the party crosses without stopping: they reach the far side safely, but one character hears their name from under the bridge later that night.
- If the party throws a rope below: the rope comes back wet, cold, and tied around a blue wax seal.
- If the party destroys part of the bridge: the echo breaks for 1 hour, revealing the hidden hook under the central rail.
Failed check:
- Investigation failed: they still notice the wagon trail, but not the hidden hook.
- Arcana failed: they know the bridge is magical, but not how the voices choose their words.

### Unexpected: Locked Room Detours
- If the party breaks the door: the room opens, but the noise alerts anyone nearby.
- If the party leaves the room alone: the clue inside is moved before they return.
- If the party tries to smoke out the room: something inside coughs once, then goes silent.
Failed check:
- Sleight of Hand failed: the lock jams, but the hinges can still be removed.
- Perception failed: they miss the second exit until someone enters the room.

### Unexpected: Social Pressure
- If the party accuses the wrong suspect: the suspect becomes defensive but reveals who gave them the false alibi.
- If the party offers a bribe: the witness accepts only if the payment is public and embarrassing.
- If the party walks away: the witness follows at a distance and tries to speak privately later.
Failed check:
- Insight failed: the witness still gives the clue, but the party cannot tell which part was exaggerated.

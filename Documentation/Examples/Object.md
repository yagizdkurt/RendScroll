# Object Examples

### Object: Broken Toll Gate
> The gate arm has been split by a single heavy impact. Iron nails jut from the wood like crooked teeth.
Image: skull

Checks:
- Investigation:
> 10: The gate was broken from the road side, not from the marsh side.
> 15: One wagon stopped here long enough for someone to unload a heavy object.
> 20: A strip of blue wax is caught under one nail.
- Survival:
> 12: Four people walked away from the wagon toward the marsh.
> 16: One set of tracks is much deeper, as if that person carried a chest or body.
- Arcana:
> 15: The blue wax carries a faint preservation charm.

Loot:
- Blue wax seal
- Bent toll spike
- Torn strip of canvas

### Object: Sealed Pantry Door
> The pantry door is swollen from damp air. A chalk circle has been drawn around the handle, and a line of salt lies unbroken across the threshold.

Checks:
- Investigation:
> 10: The door is barred from the other side.
> 15: The chalk circle has been redrawn several times.
- Religion:
> 12: The salt line is a folk ward, not a formal blessing.
> 16: The ward is meant to keep something inside the pantry.
- Athletics:
> 14: The door can be forced open, but doing so breaks the salt line.

Loot:
- Rusted pantry key
- Jar of black salt

### Object: Mirror Basin
> A shallow silver basin rests on a cracked pedestal. Its water reflects the ceiling clearly, but not the faces of anyone looking down into it.
Image: angel

Checks:
- Arcana:
> 12: The basin stores reflections instead of showing them.
> 17: Speaking a true name over the water may reveal the last person who used it.
- History:
> 15: Basins like this were used in noble houses to verify guests before private meetings.
- Insight:
> 14: The missing reflections feel selective, not broken.

Loot:
- Silver basin shard
- Damp velvet cloth
